Sector III SECTOR HEAD: X

The Western Quarter

The most populated and economically disadvantaged sector. Three vertical layers carved from stone, chaos, and survival.

Overview

Sector III is built against the dramatic cliff walls of an ancient dried river gorge that runs along its western edge. This geography defines everything about the district — its layered construction, its claustrophobic density, and the shadowy underworld that festers beneath its streets.

Once the industrial heart of Tenseishi, shifting economics left Sector III as a haven for the desperate and cunning. It is the most densely populated sector, the poorest, and the most unpredictable. No single clan dominates — instead, multiple smaller groups compete for whatever influence they can carve out in a landscape where official authority struggles to reach every corner.

The Three Layers

Cliffside Row — The High Ground

Elevated streets carved into and built along the gorge wall. A mix of structures — some literally hewn from the rock face, others constructed on ledges and platforms jutting from the cliff. This is where authority lives. The sector commander's hall sits here, along with shinobi barracks and skilled craftsmen's workshops. The higher up you are, the closer to power.

Mid-Warrens — The Surface

The sprawling, chaotic ground-level heart of the sector. Streets grew organically over generations — narrow, winding, and unpredictable. No grid, no plan, just layers of construction built over and around what came before.

The smaller clans cluster near the base of the cliff, positioning themselves between the commander above and the population below. Moving outward from the cliff, the density stays high but clan influence fades — this is pure civilian territory where the hustle of daily survival takes over.

Deep Warrens — The Undercity

Beneath the Mid-Warrens lies a labyrinth of disused aqueducts, ancient storage tunnels, and carved dwellings dating back to Tenseishi's earliest construction era. Originally infrastructure — water systems, evacuation routes, supply corridors — these tunnels were abandoned as the city evolved, then gradually repurposed by those who needed to operate outside official sight.

Access points are scattered throughout the sector — hidden doors in alley walls, old aqueduct stairways descending from basements, cave openings in the gorge face itself. There is no single entrance to seal. The network is woven into Sector III's fabric, as much a part of its architecture as the streets above.

Key Locations

Commander's Hall

Cliffside Row. Carved partially into the gorge wall, the hall overlooks the entire sector from its elevated position. The seat of Sector III's authority.

Central Plaza

Mid-Warrens. The one place where the whole community converges — announcements, trade, news, and the occasional public confrontation. The sector's social heartbeat.

Open-Air Street Markets

Mid-Warrens. Sprawling through the winding streets — food, weapons, tools, and goods of varying quality and legality. The line between legitimate trade and gray market blurs here.

Blacksmith & Craftsmen Alleys

Mid-Warrens, near the cliff base. Concentrated workshops where the sector's skilled tradespeople operate — weapon smiths, tool makers, and artisans who serve both the civilian and shinobi populations.

Tea-Houses & Gambling Dens

Mid-Warrens. Social hubs where information moves as freely as money. Some operate as straightforward businesses; others serve as fronts or neutral meeting grounds for less official dealings.

The Ancient Gorge

The dried river gorge running along the sector's western edge. Its cliff walls form the backbone of Cliffside Row and hide cave openings that connect to the Deep Warrens below.

Shinobi Barracks

Cliffside Row. Military housing and training facilities positioned on the elevated streets near the commander's hall.

Sector Gate

The western entry point connecting to the inter-sector passage network. The gateway between Sector III's chaos and the controlled corridors leading to the rest of Tenseishi.

The Deep Warrens

The undercity operates as a parallel world beneath Sector III's streets. Black market trading in weapons, intelligence, and forbidden jutsu scrolls. Smuggling routes that bypass the official sector checkpoint system entirely. An underground fight pit where shinobi test themselves for real stakes — combat with occasional death, fueled by gambling.

Secret tunnels extend beyond Sector III's borders, connecting to Sectors I and II through the ancient aqueduct infrastructure. Knowledge of these cross-sector passages is limited to those who use them — the smugglers, the buyers, and those who depend on the tunnels for business that can't happen above ground.

No single authority figure controls the Deep Warrens. The power structure remains undefined — a vacuum that functions through informal agreements, reputation, and the mutual understanding that exposure would destroy everything.

Economy & Society

Sector III's legitimate economy is built on survival — street-level trade, craftwork, and services that keep the dense population fed, equipped, and functional. The sector was once the industrial heart of Tenseishi, but shifting economics hollowed out the formal economy, leaving a population that adapted by creating its own systems of commerce — some legal, some not.

No single clan dominates. Multiple smaller groups maintain compounds near the cliff base, jockeying for whatever influence they can claim. The absence of a power monopoly means Sector III runs on pragmatism — alliances shift, loyalties are transactional, and reputation matters more than bloodline.

Command

Sector III is commanded by Raikage X, known as "Unlawful" — a self-given epithet that hints at a leader who doesn't take the formalities of power too seriously. X operates from the commander's hall on Cliffside Row, physically above the sector he governs but still discovering the full extent of what happens beneath it.

The Raikage clan descends from the historical Raikage lineage and retains the name as a family identity. X is a former jinchūriki who lost his tailed beast — a fact that makes him a commander operating at diminished power compared to his past capability. His current strength still qualifies him for the role, but the shadow of what he once was follows him.
